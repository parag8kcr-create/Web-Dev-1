1.Instagram was launched in October 2010 by Kevin Systrom and Mike Krieger.
2.Facebook (now Meta) acquired Instagram in 2012 to expand its social media ecosystem.
3.The platform allows users to share photos, videos, and Stories with followers worldwide.
4.It is widely used for social networking, brand marketing, and influencer collaborations.

1.Facebook was founded in February 2004 by Mark Zuckerberg along with Eduardo Saverin, Andrew McCollum, Dustin Moskovitz, and Chris Hughes.
2.It began as a Harvard-only network and quickly expanded to universities and then the public worldwide.
3.The platform allows users to connect, share updates, photos, videos, and join groups or events.
4.Businesses use Facebook for marketing, advertising, and building online communities.
5.Today Facebook, now under the parent company Meta, remains one of the largest social networking platforms globally.
1.Twitter was launched in March 2006 by Jack Dorsey, Biz Stone, Noah Glass, and Evan Williams.
2.It quickly became popular as a microblogging platform for sharing short messages called tweets.
3.Users post text, images, videos, and links to engage in real-time conversations.
4.It is widely used for news updates, public discussions, marketing, and live event coverage.
5.In 2022, Twitter was acquired by Elon Musk and later rebranded as X while retaining its core social features.
1.Snapchat was created in September 2011 by Evan Spiegel, Bobby Murphy, and Reggie Brown.
2.It became popular for its disappearing photo and video messages called Snaps.
3.The app introduced Stories, allowing users to share content viewable for 24 hours.
4.Brands and creators use Snapchat for marketing, filters, AR lenses, and interactive ads.
5.Today Snapchat continues to grow as a platform for quick communication and augmented reality experiences.
